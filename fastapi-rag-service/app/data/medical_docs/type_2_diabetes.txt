Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus — Clinical Reference

Definition
Type 2 diabetes is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by insulin
resistance and progressive beta-cell dysfunction, resulting in hyperglycemia.

Diagnostic Criteria (ADA)
- Fasting plasma glucose >= 126 mg/dL, OR
- 2-hour plasma glucose >= 200 mg/dL during OGTT, OR
- HbA1c >= 6.5%, OR
- Random plasma glucose >= 200 mg/dL with classic symptoms.
Prediabetes: HbA1c 5.7-6.4% or fasting glucose 100-125 mg/dL.

Classic Symptoms
Polyuria (excessive urination), polydipsia (excessive thirst), polyphagia
(increased hunger), unexplained weight loss, blurred vision, slow-healing
wounds, fatigue, recurrent infections (especially yeast and urinary).

Risk Factors
- BMI >= 25 (>= 23 in Asian populations)
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Family history in first-degree relative
- History of gestational diabetes or PCOS
- Hypertension, dyslipidemia
- Age >= 45

Treatment Ladder
1. Lifestyle: 5-10% weight loss, 150 min/week moderate exercise, Mediterranean
   or low-carb diet, smoking cessation.
2. First-line pharmacologic: metformin 500 mg BID, titrate to 1000 mg BID
   (contraindicated if eGFR < 30).
3. Add-on based on comorbidities:
   - Established ASCVD or heart failure: GLP-1 receptor agonist (semaglutide)
     or SGLT2 inhibitor (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin).
   - Chronic kidney disease: SGLT2 inhibitor preferred.
   - Cost concern: sulfonylurea (glipizide) or basal insulin.

Target HbA1c
- Most adults: < 7.0%
- Frail elderly / short life expectancy: 7.5-8.0%
- Young, low-risk: as close to 6.5% as safely achievable

Complications to Screen For
- Retinopathy: annual dilated eye exam
- Nephropathy: annual urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio, eGFR
- Neuropathy: annual monofilament foot exam
- Cardiovascular: lipid panel yearly, aspirin if 10-yr ASCVD risk > 10%

Hypoglycemia Warning
Insulin and sulfonylureas can cause dangerously low blood sugar. Symptoms:
sweating, tremor, confusion, palpitations. Treat with 15 g fast-acting carbs
(4 oz juice, 3-4 glucose tabs) and recheck in 15 minutes.
