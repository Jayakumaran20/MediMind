Adult Vaccination Schedule (CDC 2024 — routine)

Influenza (Flu)
Annual; all persons >= 6 months. High-dose or adjuvanted formulations
preferred for age >= 65. Optimal timing: September-October.

COVID-19
Annual updated vaccine per current CDC guidance for all age >= 6 months.
Additional dose for immunocompromised.

Tdap / Td
Tdap once in adulthood, then Td or Tdap booster every 10 years.
Tdap during every pregnancy (27-36 weeks gestation).

Pneumococcal (PCV20 or PCV15 + PPSV23)
- All adults >= 65
- Age 19-64 with chronic conditions (heart, lung, liver, diabetes, smoking,
  immunocompromise, CSF leaks, cochlear implants)

Zoster (Shingrix, RZV)
Two doses 2-6 months apart. All adults >= 50. Immunocompromised >= 19.

RSV
Single dose for adults >= 75, or 60-74 with high risk. Also for pregnant
persons 32-36 weeks gestation to protect newborn.

HPV (Gardasil 9)
Routine ages 9-26. Shared clinical decision-making ages 27-45.

Hepatitis B
All adults 19-59; older with risk factors. Three-dose or two-dose schedule
depending on product.

MMR
Two doses for adults born in or after 1957 without immunity.

Meningococcal
Age-specific and risk-based (college dorm, military, asplenia, complement
deficiency, HIV, travel to meningitis belt).

Pharmacy Administration Notes
- Verify patient age, condition, contraindications (severe allergy to
  component, immunocompromise for live vaccines).
- Standing orders or prescriber protocol required per state.
- Document via IIS (Immunization Information System / state registry).
- Observe patient 15 min post-injection (30 min if history of anaphylaxis).
