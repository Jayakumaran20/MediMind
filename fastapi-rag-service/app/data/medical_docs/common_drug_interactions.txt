Common Clinically Significant Drug-Drug Interactions

Warfarin (Coumadin)
- + NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen): increased bleeding risk
- + antibiotics (metronidazole, TMP-SMX, fluoroquinolones): elevated INR
- + amiodarone: 2-fold INR increase — reduce warfarin dose 30-50%
- + acetaminophen > 2 g/day chronic: mild INR elevation
Action: monitor INR closely, adjust dose, consider PPI for GI protection.

Statins (atorvastatin, simvastatin)
- + macrolides (clarithromycin, erythromycin): rhabdomyolysis risk via CYP3A4
- + grapefruit juice: increased statin levels (simvastatin especially)
- + fibrates (gemfibrozil): myopathy risk
Action: hold simvastatin during short antibiotic courses; use pravastatin
or rosuvastatin (non-CYP3A4) in polypharmacy patients.

SSRIs / SNRIs
- + MAOIs: serotonin syndrome (potentially fatal) — 14-day washout required
- + tramadol, linezolid, triptans, St John's Wort: serotonin syndrome risk
- + NSAIDs: increased GI bleeding risk
Symptoms of serotonin syndrome: hyperthermia, hyperreflexia, clonus,
agitation, autonomic instability.

Metformin
- + iodinated contrast media: hold 48h before and after in CKD patients
  (risk of lactic acidosis)
- + alcohol (heavy): lactic acidosis
Action: check eGFR before contrast studies.

ACE Inhibitors
- + potassium supplements or K-sparing diuretics (spironolactone): hyperkalemia
- + NSAIDs: reduced antihypertensive effect + acute kidney injury
- + lithium: elevated lithium levels

Digoxin
- + amiodarone, verapamil, clarithromycin: increased digoxin levels
- + loop diuretics: hypokalemia potentiates digoxin toxicity
Toxicity signs: nausea, visual disturbances (yellow halos), arrhythmia.

QT-Prolonging Combinations (Torsades de Pointes risk)
Avoid combinations of: amiodarone, sotalol, methadone, ondansetron,
haloperidol, fluoroquinolones, macrolides, citalopram > 20 mg in elderly.

Opioids + Benzodiazepines
FDA black box warning: profound sedation, respiratory depression, death.
Avoid coprescribing when possible.
