Asthma — Clinical Overview

Definition
Asthma is a chronic inflammatory disorder of the airways causing recurrent
episodes of wheezing, breathlessness, chest tightness, and coughing due to
variable airflow obstruction and bronchial hyperresponsiveness.

Common Triggers
- Allergens: dust mites, pet dander, pollen, mold
- Respiratory infections (viral URIs)
- Exercise, cold air
- Tobacco smoke, air pollution
- Occupational exposures (isocyanates, flour dust)
- NSAIDs and beta-blockers (in sensitive individuals)
- Emotional stress

Severity Classification (GINA)
- Intermittent: symptoms < 2x/week, nighttime awakenings < 2x/month
- Mild persistent: symptoms > 2x/week but not daily
- Moderate persistent: daily symptoms, nighttime > 1x/week
- Severe persistent: continuous symptoms, frequent nighttime awakenings

Stepwise Treatment (GINA 2023)
Step 1: As-needed low-dose ICS-formoterol (preferred) or SABA + low-dose ICS
Step 2: Daily low-dose ICS (e.g., budesonide 200-400 mcg) or as-needed ICS-formoterol
Step 3: Low-dose ICS-LABA (formoterol/budesonide)
Step 4: Medium-dose ICS-LABA
Step 5: Add-on LAMA (tiotropium) or biologics (omalizumab, mepolizumab)

Acute Exacerbation Management
- Mild-moderate: SABA (albuterol) 4-10 puffs via spacer every 20 min for 1 hour
- Add oral corticosteroids (prednisone 40-50 mg daily x 5-7 days) for moderate+
- Severe: seek emergency care — signs include inability to speak in full
  sentences, silent chest, cyanosis, altered mental status, PEF < 50% predicted.

Peak Flow Monitoring
- Green zone (80-100% personal best): continue routine treatment
- Yellow zone (50-79%): add SABA, may need oral steroids
- Red zone (< 50%): use rescue inhaler, seek emergency care

Patient Education Essentials
- Correct inhaler technique (spacer recommended for MDI)
- Written asthma action plan
- Trigger avoidance
- Annual influenza vaccination
