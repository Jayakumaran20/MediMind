Retail Pharmacy Prescription Workflow (CVS-style Operations)

Prescription Intake
Prescriptions arrive via e-prescribe (Surescripts), fax, phone, or paper.
E-prescribe is preferred: legibility, DEA validation, patient-matched.

Data Verification Steps
1. Patient identity: full name, DOB, address must match profile.
2. Prescriber DEA number required for controlled substances (schedules II-V).
3. Drug: name, strength, dosage form, quantity, days supply.
4. Directions (Sig): must be clinically sensible; flag "take as directed" for follow-up.
5. Refill count: 0-5 for non-controlled; C-II has no refills.

Insurance Adjudication
Real-time claim sent to PBM via NCPDP D.0 telecom standard.
Common rejects:
- 70 (Product/Service not covered)
- 75 (Prior authorization required)
- 88 (DUR reject — drug utilization review)
- 79 (Refill too soon)

Drug Utilization Review (DUR) Checks
- Drug-drug interactions
- Duplicate therapy
- High dose alerts
- Allergy checks
- Pregnancy warnings
- Age-inappropriate medications (Beers criteria for elderly)

Fill Process
1. Robotic or manual dispensing per NDC number.
2. Label print with patient name, drug, directions, warnings, prescriber, RX#.
3. Auxiliary labels (e.g., "Take with food", "May cause drowsiness").
4. Pharmacist final verification: visual match, DUR review, counseling flag.

Patient Counseling
Federal law (OBRA-90) requires offer of counseling for new prescriptions.
Topics: name & purpose, dose & timing, common side effects, missed dose,
storage, refills. Documented via signature or e-signature.

Adherence Programs
- ReadyFill (auto-refill)
- 90-day fills for chronic maintenance meds
- Medication Therapy Management (MTM) for Medicare Part D patients
  with multiple chronic conditions

Common Data Quality Issues
- NDC mismatch after manufacturer change
- Days supply calculation errors for topicals and inhalers
- Duplicate profiles from spelling variants
- Missing prescriber NPI on legacy scripts
